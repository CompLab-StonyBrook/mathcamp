\noindent
The Ilocano pattern is:
%
\begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{rcl}
        ka & bigat & an\\
        kaka & bigat & anan\\
        kakaka& bigat & ananan\\
        kakakaka& bigat & anananan\\
        & $\vdots$\\
    \end{tabular}
\end{center}
%
We can write this more succinctly as $\text{ka}^n\ \text{bigat}\ \text{an}^n$ ($n \geq 0$).
